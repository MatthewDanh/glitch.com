Interplay of Capitalism & Democracy

An Essay by Matthew Nichols

Abstract

This essay examines the complex relationship between capitalism and democracy in the United States. It highlights historical injustices, including slavery, Indigenous displacement, and modern-day child labor, while exploring the ongoing struggle to align these two systems' principles.

Key Insights

  • Slavery: A system of economic exploitation that prioritized profits over human dignity and democratic ideals.
  • Indigenous Displacement: Policies like the Indian Removal Act facilitated capitalist expansion at the expense of Native sovereignty.
  • Japanese Internment: Civil liberties were overridden by political and economic fears during WWII.
  • LGBTQ+ Rights: Historic and ongoing struggles illustrate gaps in equal protection under the law.
  • Child Labor: Economic systems often exploit marginalized groups, with children in agriculture facing dangerous conditions.

Conclusion

The relationship between capitalism and democracy is fraught with contradictions but offers opportunities for reform. By addressing systemic injustices and fostering inclusive policies, the United States can align its economic practices with its democratic ideals.
